## Prompt `interactive`

**Question:** what is cardiac arrhythmia?

### Base model

Cardiac arrhythmia, also known as an irregular heartbeat, is a condition where the heart beats irregularly, either too quickly, too slowly, or with an irregular rhythm. It occurs when the electrical signals that control the heartbeat become disrupted, causing the heart to beat in an abnormal pattern.

There are several types of cardiac arrhythmias, including:

1. **Atrial fibrillation**: The upper chambers of the heart (atria) beat too quickly and irregularly, often causing a rapid, irregular heartbeat.
2. **Atrial flutter**: The upper chambers of the heart beat too quickly and regularly, but in an irregular pattern.
3. **Ventricular tachycardia**: The lower chambers of the heart (ventricles) beat too quickly and irregularly, often causing a rapid heartbeat.
4. **Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT)**: The electrical signals that control the heartbeat are disrupted, causing a rapid heartbeat that originates from above the ventricles.
5. **Bradycardia**: The heart beats too slowly, often below 60 beats per minute.
6. **Tachycardia**: The heart beats too quickly, often above 100 beats per minute.

Causes of cardiac arrhythmias can include:

1. **Heart disease**: Conditions such as coronary artery disease, heart valve problems, and cardiomyopathy.
2. **Electrolyte imbalances**: Abnormal levels of electrolytes such as potassium, sodium, and magnesium.
3. **Medications**: Certain medications, such as beta blockers
